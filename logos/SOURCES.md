# Logo Sources

All files below were sourced from Wikimedia Commons, which hosts public-domain works of
U.S. federal employees (17 U.S.C. § 105). Each candidate was rejected if its Commons title,
page description, or category contained "seal," AND — going one step further — each surviving
candidate was rendered/viewed and visually compared against known seal designs already present
in this directory before being downloaded. Two candidates that passed the text-based "seal"
filter were rejected anyway after visual inspection proved they were the seal in substance
(see Rejected table below).

## Coast Guard: NONE FOUND — no non-seal mark exists on Commons

Every discoverable Coast Guard organizational mark on Wikimedia Commons — regardless of whether its
filename said "seal," "emblem," or "mark" — turned out, on visual inspection, to embed the same
circular device: crossed anchors, a shield, a ring reading "UNITED STATES COAST GUARD," and the
founding date "1790." Per the binding rule ("reject anything with a circular device ring carrying
the service name, [or] a founding date"), every candidate found was rejected. No file exists on
Commons depicting the bare shield-on-crossed-anchors without that ring, and the "racing stripe"
variants all have the same ringed device inset into the stripe rather than standing alone. Nothing
was downloaded for Coast Guard.
